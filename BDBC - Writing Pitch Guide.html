2. OPENING SENTENCE ‚ñº
Your opening sentence should do TWO things:
  1. Tell the editor what the email is about - who the brand is and what the product is
  2. Tell the editor why they should care - why the product is worth writing about
‚öôÔ∏è FORMATTING RULES:
Have you pitched the editor this client MORE than six times?
‚úì Use the brand name without brand descriptor (because you can assume, at this point, they know who the brand is)
Buck Mason just added new styles and colors to its Featherweight Silk Cashmere Collection‚Ä¶
Have you pitched the editor this client LESS than six times?
‚úì Add brand descriptor
The Los Angeles-based essentials brand, Buck Mason, has just added new styles and colors to its Featherweight Silk Cashmere Collection...
Are you pitching a collection that has a name?
‚úì Provide a single sentence description of the collection (collection names are essentially a meaningless combination of words, so the editors need more info)
The Los Angeles-based essentials brand, Buck Mason just added new styles to its Featherweight Silk Cashmere Collection, a range of ribbed tops made from a sumptuous merino / silk / cashmere blend.
Are you pitching more than three products together, but they aren't technically a collection with a name?
‚úì Use your opening sentence to explain why the products are being grouped together, as well as tell the editor why they should care
Reigning Champ has expanded its collection RDS-certified down outerwear with new styles and colors.
Are you pitching just one or two products?
‚úì Simple introduction format
Sharing the brand new Transit Tote from Cleverpup - a feature-rich dog carrier that also doubles as an everyday bag. Sharing a closer look at KESTIN's Dunbar Padded Jacket and Pathhead Puffer Vest, two vintage-inspired outerwear styles that are as sustainable as they are warm.
Are the products net new? / Are the products old but back-in-stock or available in new colors?
If products are old but back-in-stock or available in new colors, mention that detail
